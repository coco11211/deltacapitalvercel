    Risk & Controls

    Risk is not managed.
    It is understood.

    Risk management is not a function within our process. It is the process. Every model output, every allocation decision, every execution parameter is evaluated through the lens of controlled exposure.

    Framework

    Failure-First Design

    We design systems assuming they will fail. Every risk parameter is calibrated not for expected conditions, but for the conditions that would be most destructive. This asymmetric framing is deliberate.

    Our risk framework operates across multiple horizons and granularities: position-level, strategy-level, and portfolio-level. Limits are enforced programmatically, without override capability during live trading.

    Measurement

    Quantifying the Left Tail

    Value at Risk

    Portfolio-level VaR is computed using parametric, historical simulation, and Monte Carlo methods. Divergence between methods serves as an additional risk signal.

    Conditional Value at Risk

    CVaR captures expected loss beyond the VaR threshold, providing a coherent measure of tail exposure that informs position sizing and hedging decisions.

    Maximum Drawdown

    Real-time drawdown monitoring with automated deleveraging triggers. Historical drawdown profiles are used to calibrate strategy-level capital allocation.

    Stress Testing

    Systematic stress scenarios are applied continuously, not episodically. Scenarios include historical crises, hypothetical dislocations, and reverse stress tests.

    Controls

    Programmatic Discipline

    Pre-Trade Validation

    Every order passes through position limits, concentration limits, margin requirements, and regulatory compliance checks before reaching the execution layer.

    Real-Time Monitoring

    Continuous monitoring of exposure, P&L, liquidity metrics, and system health. Anomaly detection triggers automated protective actions.

    Immutable Audit Trail

    Every action—order, fill, cancellation, risk check, parameter change—is logged with microsecond precision in append-only storage.

    The firm that thinks about failure first
    is the firm that endures.
