    FAQ

    Common Questions

    What does Delta Capital do?

    We build and operate quantitative trading systems. Our models generate systematic investment signals from statistical analysis of market data. We do not make discretionary bets.

    What markets do you trade?

    Primarily liquid equities and equity derivatives across major global exchanges. We focus on markets with sufficient depth and transparency to support systematic execution.

    What is your investment horizon?

    Intraday to multi-week. Our strategies span multiple holding periods, from short-term mean reversion to medium-term momentum. We do not operate at high-frequency timescales and we do not hold positions for months.

    How do you manage risk?

    Programmatically. Risk limits are enforced by the system, not by human judgment. Position limits, concentration limits, drawdown triggers, and exposure caps are computed in real-time and operate without manual override during live trading sessions.

    Do you accept outside capital?

    Selectively. We prioritize alignment over scale. Inquiries regarding investment should be directed to the contact page. We do not respond to unsolicited pitches.

    What is your track record?

    We do not publish performance figures on this website. Verified track record information is available to qualified parties under non-disclosure agreement. Past performance is not indicative of future results.

    What technology do you use?

    Our infrastructure is proprietary. We do not disclose specific technology choices, programming languages, or cloud providers. What we will say: we optimize for reliability and reproducibility over speed. Our systems are deterministic by design.

    Are you hiring?

    We do not maintain open positions. When we hire, we reach out directly. If you believe you should be on our radar, send a brief note through the contact page. No resumes. No cover letters. Just a clear statement of what you do well.

    Why is your website so minimal?

    Because the work speaks for itself. We allocate our design budget to systems that generate returns, not to systems that generate impressions.
